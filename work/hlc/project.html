Risk audit app

Insurance

Overview

I was asked to design an app for insurance agents, who had to do hazard identification and risk assessment for companies across different markets. The risk assessments were to be performed through buildings and processes audits.

Audits were performed in front of the competition (other insurance companies) in the same room. Everyone was asking questions for their surveys (on different topics). Therefore, switching between different parts of the survey was a key skill. The questionnaire consisted of around 1500 questions, with 4 tiers of hierarchy.

Team

  • Lead designer (me)
  • Visual Designer
  • 2 iOS developers (font)
  • Back-end developer

Challenges

  • Waterfall

    Working in this framework made a lot of siloed work, which was slowing down everyone's work

  • Experts

The process

Research

Users: Group of specialists (fire protection, electrical engineering, building engineering, and more) taking care of corporate clients. Around a dozen people from over a five hundred employees company.

Since our users were highly specialised in the task they were performing, I had to be sure I understood their job perfectly. I wanted to make their work easier and faster, so I had to avoid making any assumptions.

Methods

  • Few iterations of workshops/interviews with stakeholders and key-specialists to set project vision
  • Card-sorting session to break questionnaires into smaller chunks, easier for agents to digest
  • Interviews with users to set tasks they were performing

Research outcome - main goals

Stand out from the competition
The survey was conducted in a group meeting with competition in the same room. Everyone was asking different questions, about a different part of the company. Competitors were mostly using Excel documents on their laptops with and similar document templates - just like our

Be lightning fast
Since the survey was conducted in a group, our user had to be fast enough to switch from topic to topic. Other surveyors weren’t helpful by asking different questions for different parts of the production

Include photos
Our users were taking a lot of photos - documents, certifications, machinery, etc.

They needed to switch fast between taking a photo and conducting a survey. Some of them should be included in the final report of the questionnaire

Around 1500 questions
This is a lot of questions that could be asked. By a lot, I mean a lot.

Deliverables

  • Lo-fi sketches
  • Mid and Hi-fi mock-ups (Photoshop, Sketch)
  • User-flows, diagrams
  • InVision prototypes
  • app-map
  • Full app analysis (approx. 60-page Document) that was used by developers
  • Feature list

Phase 3 - Ideation sessions

I did ideation sessions with stakeholders and few agents at client headquarters.

Goals

To generate different solutions that we could test with prototypes.

Results

• iPad Pro app

Imagine one of the agents pulling out their iPad Pro which was instantaneously ready, while others had to take out their laptops, power cables, mice, cameras, etc. We were sure that the new way of working would be standing out from what competition had.

iPad was also great at taking photos, so agents wouldn't have to take additional pieces of equipment with them. The stylus was an extra feature that could help our users making fast sketches (i.e., on photos). Therefore, all the work could be done on one device - no memory cards, no switching between different apps, devices, etc.

  • The survey was based on switch components

    To speed up work I came up with the switch-based screen, which only would need 1 tap to “check”

  • Showing deeper levels only when parent switch was turned on

    I wanted to make sure UI is not cluttered with unnecessary questions. Thanks to having only top-level questions for a new project, our users were presented with a clear UI that could be expanded during the questionnaire process.

Phase 4 - Prototypes

The most challenging screen was the one with the survey. I iterated many times to make it highly usable and aesthetically pleasing.

Goals

To make sure interactions are easy to understand and perform, and to improve the structure of the surveys

Results

• 6 iteration of survey screens

• Polished interactions, so every agent could do them easily • List of additional features to include in next version

Phase 5 - Tests

Goals

To choose the best solution for our users

Results

• Changed user-flow to match users’ expectations

• Changed list of features to include in the first release (few of them were moved to

next versions)

Phase 6 - Implement

During implementation, I worked hand-in-hand with developers to make sure everything worked as intended. We shared the early-beta version of the app with our users, so we could receive feedback (if any errors occurred, API connection worked, etc.) and pivot if needed.

Summary

  • Application proudly presented at board meetings
  • High users’ satisfaction
  • 1 tool to work with (in contrast to laptop, camera, many apps)
  • Agents standing out from the competition
  • Much easier survey completion and switching between topics
